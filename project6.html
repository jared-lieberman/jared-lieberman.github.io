Investigating Voter Turnout in Indian Elections

The purpose of this analysis is to use Bayesian multilevel models to explore potential factors of why voter turnout tends to be higher in local and state-level elections vs. national elections. India generally has a comparatively higher turnout rate, and is unique in that voter turnout increases as each level of election decreases. The United States and Europe, where most of the turnout research has been conducted, face the opposite effects. I used election data from 1967-2021, and explored some potential explanations like which type of parties win at what rates of turnout, if there is an effect in the timing of state and national elections, and what regional differences exist.

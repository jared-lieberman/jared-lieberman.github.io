Transportability and External Validity in Evaluating Women's Political Reservation Effects in India

This project aims to examine the generalizability of causal effects obtained from a randomized controlled trial (RCT), specifically, the “Women as Policy Makers” (Duflo et. al.) study in India, to a new target population. Using the structural causal model (SCM) framework, the project will build a formal SCM for the RCT data. Next, methods from transportability and external validity, for example selection diagrams, will determine whether the estimated effect (e.g., the impact of reserved political positions on local public good investments) can be identified in a new population that differs in key covariates (such as socio-economic characteristics or cultural factors). The project focuses on generalizability, robustness, and adaptation. Using data from the RCT as well as additional relevant data from external sources, the project aims to bridge the gap between rigorous experimental findings and policy decisions in varied real-world environments.

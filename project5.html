Mapping Afro-Brazilian Religious Histories: An AI-Assisted Tutorial

I was selected as a Data Science Institute Scholar at Columbia University to work on an interdisciplinary NLP-based project with the Center for Spatial Research and Department of Latin American and Iberian Cultures. This project examined the historical and spatial dimensions of witchcraft accusations in Rio de Janeiro during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. By combining archival research with data science techniques, such as web scraping, natural language processing (NLP), and geospatial mapping, the project helped to explore how accusations of witchcraft were used to reinforce racial and class divides. Using the addresses of accused witches, the project aims to visualize how these trials mapped onto the city, reinforcing social and spatial segregation. We started the process with a glossary of about 60 relevant terms and performed searches across newspapers including Jornal do Brasil, Gazeta de Notícias, Diário de Pernambuco, and Diário da Noite.

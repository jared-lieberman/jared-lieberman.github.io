About Me

I am a data scientist and interdisciplinary quantitative researcher, currently studying in the Statistics MA program at Columbia University. My interests are in causal inference, experimentation, and machine learning. I also work in quantitative research at Eurasia Group, a political risk research and advisory firm, where I contribute methodological expertise and support data science efforts.

Prior to graduate school, I was a Fulbright-Nehru Research Fellow in India, where I conducted mixed-methods research on state-level elections and voter behavior. I was affiliated with the Trivedi Centre for Political Data at Ashoka University, and performed research at the Centre for Policy Research.

I have also worked as a data scientist on a research and development team within the Department of Revenue at the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, a software developer at MIT, and a technical intern at Evidence for Policy Design, housed within the Harvard Center for International Development.

Awards and Fellowships

My work at the Massachusetts state government was recognized with the Commonwealth Citation for Outstanding Performance Award. I was also granted the Critical Language Enhancement Award by The Fulbright Program to fund my Hindi-language education.

In the summer of 2020, I was selected as part of the inaugural cohort of the NYC[x] Innovation Fellowship. While at Columbia, I conducted geospatial research using NLP as a Data Science Institute Scholar with the Center for Spatial Research.
